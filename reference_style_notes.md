# Reference Style Notes

Source folder: `E:\例图`

## Core Direction

These references center on Chinese traditional auspicious animal ornament:

- Deer antler motifs for "福禄" and "鹿角".
- Baoxiang-style sheep and tiger patterns with mirrored ornamental curls.
- Ancient beast-head and guardian-animal motifs.
- Han dynasty image-stone / rubbing references: tiger, white tiger, winged beast, dragon-like animal silhouettes.
- Folk embroidery and tiger-pillow language: bright, bold, symmetrical, talismanic.

## Visual DNA

- Structure: strong bilateral symmetry, central vertical axis, paired animals or paired horns, poster-like centered layout.
- Shape language: S-curves, hooked cloud curls, flame tips, ruyi-like arcs, long sweeping tails, antler branches, leaf and flower tendrils.
- Animal treatment: stylized rather than realistic; faces are mask-like, frontal or side-profile, with exaggerated eyes, teeth, paws, horns, whiskers, and striped bodies.
- Linework: thick outer contour, thinner inner division lines, hand-drawn unevenness, rubbing-like broken edges, visible wear and texture.
- Pattern fill: flat color blocks, layered inner shapes, repeated stripes, dots, scallops, and leaf/flame segments.
- Texture: aged rice-paper background, stone-rubbing grain, embroidery-thread feeling, distressed print surfaces.

## Color Palette

Use an aged traditional palette rather than glossy digital color:

- Background: warm rice paper, beige, parchment, old ivory.
- Main colors: muted teal, stone blue, cinnabar red, dark wine red, clay brown, moss green, faded mint, ochre, coral, cream white.
- High-energy variant: deep red background with lighter red/orange pattern, subtle woodgrain or wave texture.
- Avoid neon, pure gradients, chrome effects, and overly clean vector polish.

## Composition Patterns

- Single emblem: one animal or antler motif centered on parchment with generous empty space.
- Pattern sheet: multiple motif variants arranged around a central vertical title plaque.
- Ancient study sheet: black-and-white rubbings or line drawings, with rough scanned edges and uneven ink density.
- Folk poster: red field, thin decorative border, large beast-head motifs, strong auspicious character.
- Product/object direction: embroidered tiger pillow style, saturated colors, tactile fabric texture.

## Prompt Keywords

Chinese traditional auspicious animal ornament, Han dynasty stone rubbing inspiration, baoxiang pattern, folk embroidery, tiger pillow, deer antler motif, guardian beast, symmetrical totem, ruyi cloud curls, aged parchment texture, muted mineral colors, hand-drawn distressed ink edges, cinnabar red and teal, vintage Chinese poster layout.

## Design Rules For Future Work

- Keep the silhouette readable first; details should decorate the body, not destroy recognition.
- Use symmetry for ceremonial/emblem designs; use side-profile motion for Han-rubbing animal designs.
- Prefer flat mineral colors with distressed edges over smooth modern gradients.
- Let negative space breathe on parchment layouts.
- Use calligraphic Chinese titles or seal marks only when they serve the design.
- If making new motifs, merge animal anatomy with cloud, flame, leaf, and ruyi forms.
- If vectorizing, preserve imperfect hand-cut edges and rubbing grain where possible.
